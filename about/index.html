Alyson Hurt

Alyson Hurt is a senior interactive designer with NPR's news applications team, working on infographics, interactives and special news presentations. She previously worked at washingtonpost.com and azcentral.com. She holds an M.A. in Communication, Culture and Technology from Georgetown University and a B.A. in Journalism from Arizona State University. Outside of work, she enjoys letterpress and bicycling. She lives in Arlington, Va., with her husband Rob and their two rabbits, Milo and Lily. Find her on Twitter as @alykat or send email to ahurt@npr.org

Christopher Groskopf

Christopher Groskopf is a News Applications Developer at NPR. Before joining NPR he was lead developer on the PANDA Project and a member of the Chicago Tribune's News Applications team. He is the maintainer of several open source projects, including Hack Tyler and csvkit. He lives in Tyler, TX with his son and listens to his coding music at unreasonable volumes. Find him on Twitter as @onyxfish or send email to cgroskopf@npr.org.
