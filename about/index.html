About Our Team

Brian Boyer

Brian Boyer is NPR's news applications editor. He came to NPR from the Chicago Tribune, where he led a similar team of data journalists, designers and developers who built news applications and editorial tools. He loves his job. Find him on Twitter as @brianboyer or email bboyer@npr.org.

Jeremy Bowers

Jeremy Bowers is a news applications developer. Before NPR, Jeremy worked for The Washington Post and the (former) St. Petersburg Times. Jeremy loves baseball, barbecue, his wife @beckybowers and his lovely daughter, #TheSqueezle. Find him on Twitter as @jeremybowers or email jbowers@npr.org.

Danny DeBelius

Danny DeBelius is a news applications designer. He was previously a senior user experience designer for the StateImpact project. Prior to joining NPR, Danny worked in the newsrooms of the Las Vegas Sun and Rocky Mountain News. Find him on Twitter as @dannydb or email ddebelius@npr.org.

Christopher Groskopf

Christopher Groskopf is a news applications developer. Before joining NPR he was lead developer on the PANDA Project and a member of the Chicago Tribune News Applications team. He is the maintainer of several open source projects, including Hack Tyler and csvkit. He lives in Tyler, TX with his son and listens to his coding music at unreasonable volumes. Find him on Twitter as @onyxfish or email cgroskopf@npr.org.

Alyson Hurt

Alyson Hurt is a news applications designer, working on infographics, interactives and special news presentations. She previously worked at washingtonpost.com and azcentral.com. She holds an M.A. in Communication, Culture and Technology from Georgetown University and a B.A. in Journalism from Arizona State University. Outside of work, she enjoys letterpress and bicycling. She lives in Arlington, Va., with her husband Rob and their two rabbits, Milo and Lily. Find her on Twitter as @alykat or email ahurt@npr.org

Matt Stiles

Matt Stiles is the team's data editor. He previously was the database reporting coordinator for NPR's StateImpact project. Before joining NPR, Stiles worked as a reporter and data applications editor at The Texas Tribune, a digital news startup in Austin. He has also covered politics and law enforcement at The Dallas Morning News and the Houston Chronicle. Find him on Twitter at @stiles or email mstiles@npr.org.
